# H-001: Pullback Depth Predicts Trade Outcome

## Prediction
Deeper pullbacks (more negative `ema_distance` at entry) produce better
expectancy than shallow ones, WITHIN THE OBSERVED RANGE. Rationale:
shallow pullbacks are noise entries mid-impulse; deep pullbacks buy
discount within an intact trend.

## Directional claim
Expectancy is monotonically increasing as `ema_distance` moves from the
shallowest observed quartile toward the deepest observed quartile,
across all three instruments — bounded to the TRAIN-derived quartile
range, not extrapolated beyond it.

Explicitly NOT claimed: that the relationship continues indefinitely
past the deepest observed quartile. At extreme depth (roughly -4 ATR
and beyond, per Fix #9-regenerated data) a pullback may reflect trend
failure rather than discount-buying, which would produce a U-shape
(deep-but-not-extreme wins) rather than a straight monotonic line. A
U-shaped result inside the claimed range is treated as a DISTINCT,
weaker finding — not an automatic refutation of "deeper is better than
shallow" — but the STRICT monotonic version of the claim is considered
killed if the best bin is not the deepest bin. This distinction is
written down now, before any data is looked at, specifically so it
cannot be renegotiated after seeing results.

## Test design
- Data: fresh M0 trade logs (669 trades, regenerated 2026-07-18 post
  Fix #9 / entry-feature-capture / coupling fix), TRAIN window only
  (2019–2022) for binning; bins frozen before OOS look.
- Bins: `ema_distance` quartiles computed on TRAIN, per instrument
  (quartile boundaries are data-derived, not fixed a priori — this is
  itself a registered choice and will not be changed after looking at
  OOS).
- Metric: expectancy/trade and PF per bin.
- Null: same binning applied to `null_random.py` trades (M2 dependency)
  — interim null until then: shuffle `ema_distance` labels 1,000x,
  permutation p-value.

## Kill criteria (any one refutes the STRICT monotonic claim)
- Direction inconsistent across instruments (e.g., deep best on
  USDJPY, worst on GBPJPY).
- Best-vs-worst bin expectancy gap not significant vs. permutation
  null (p > 0.05).
- Fewer than 30 OOS trades in the claimed-best bin -> verdict:
  INSUFFICIENT EVIDENCE (not FAIL — a distinct verdict class).
- Effect vanishes under cost +50% stress.
- Best bin is not the deepest quartile (kills the STRICT monotonic
  version specifically; a U-shape with the second-deepest quartile
  winning is logged as a separate, weaker "bounded discount zone"
  finding, not silently folded into a pass).

## What I will NOT do
- Re-bin after seeing OOS results.
- Extend the directional claim past the deepest observed TRAIN
  quartile.
- Test `trend_gap` in this registration — that is a separate,
  future hypothesis (H-002 candidate).
- Relabel a U-shaped result as a monotonic pass after the fact.
